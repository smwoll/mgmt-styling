Office of Management

The Office of Management supports Peace Corps world-wide through the offices of Administrative Services, the Freedom of Information Act, Privacy Act, Records Management, and the Office of Staff Learning and Development.

Management Divisions

Administrative Services

Administrative Services provides direct support to Headquarters, Regional offices, and Peace Corps posts by ordering medical supplies and equipment, purchasing or leasing office supplies and property, and facilitating travel of staff and Volunteers.

The Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) and Privacy Act Office

The Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) and Privacy Act Office provides support to the Agency world-wide by providing guidance and assistance in the processing of FOIA and Privacy Act requests. The FOIA Office provides that any person has a right to obtain access to federal agency records unless such records (or portions of them) are protected from public disclosure. The Privacy Act establishes a code of fair information practices that governs the collection, maintenance, use, and dissemination of personally identifiable information about individuals that is maintained in systems of records by federal agencies. The Privacy Act Office processes requests for individuals and manages Privacy Threshold Analysis (PTA), Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA), System of Records Notices (SORN). This office also manages Peace Corps forms policies and procedures. The Form Management sub-unit applies to all Peace Corps offices and all forms created by the Peace Corps.

Records Management

Records Management oversees the maintenance and legal disposition of records created or received by Peace Corps staff in the conduct of official business. Records can be in any media or format, including paper, audiovisual, cartographic, or electronic.
